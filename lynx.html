Lynx

Although they closely resemble the bobcat, the Lynx's tail is completley black-tipped, while the bobcat's tail has a white ring below the black tip.

  • Scientific Name: Lynx Canadensis Kerr
  • Average Length: 90 centimeters
  • Average Lifespan: 15 years
  • Habitat: Boreal forests that have cold, snowy winters.

The Lynx is a very solitary and mysterious animal. You can track a Lynx for a month and never see it. The Lynx has a wide face with fluffy patches that make the Lynx appear bearded. The Lynx is more than twice the size of a house cat. They are excellent night hunters and prefer to hunt the snowshoe hare.
